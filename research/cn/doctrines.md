# China — Strategic doctrines shaping cyber tasking

Doctrines are listed in approximate descending order of operational relevance to attributed cyber activity. Each entry follows the AUSPEX SCHEMA. Citations are numbered per-entry; primary Chinese-government text is preferred where it exists.

---

### Made in China 2025 (中国制造2025 / MIC2025)

- **Issued**: 2015 by the State Council (issued 19 May 2015, drafted by MIIT under instruction of the State Council; signed by Premier Li Keqiang).
- **Official text**: <https://english.www.gov.cn/policies/latest_releases/2015/05/19/content_281475110703534.htm> (State Council release in English). Chinese full text via Gov.cn / MIIT.
- **Status**: active. Public messaging downplayed after U.S. tariff pressure (2018), but targets and funding mechanisms continued through Big Fund III and the 14th FYP.
- **Cyber-relevant pillars**:
  - Achieve self-sufficiency in ten strategic sectors by 2025; "core" component localization 70% by 2025.
  - Catch-up via "indigenous innovation" — explicitly authorizes overseas acquisition, talent recruitment, and technology absorption.
  - Designates semiconductors / integrated circuits as the foundational bottleneck.
- **Sectors / targets implied (the "ten sectors")**: (1) New-generation IT (including semiconductors, 5G, software); (2) high-end CNC machine tools & robotics; (3) aerospace & aeronautical equipment; (4) maritime engineering equipment & high-tech ships; (5) advanced rail equipment; (6) new-energy vehicles & equipment; (7) power equipment; (8) agricultural equipment; (9) new materials; (10) biopharma & advanced medical products. [1][2]
- **Timeline pressure**: 2025 milestone (10-year horizon from 2015 issuance).
- **Summary**: MIC2025 is the master industrial-policy document that frames almost every observed PRC commercial-IP cyber theft campaign of the past decade. It commands self-sufficiency in ten specifically enumerated sectors and explicitly endorses absorbing foreign technology where domestic R&D lags. The doctrine maps directly to attested cyber targeting patterns: APT10 against managed service providers serving aerospace and pharma; APT41 against semiconductor and gaming firms; APT40 against maritime engineering and biotech; Boyusec/APT3 against Siemens (power equipment) and Moody's (financial data underpinning industrial strategy). When a Chinese cyber operation hits a target in one of the ten sectors with no other obvious explanation, MIC2025 is the default doctrine linkage.
- **Citations**:
  1. State Council, "'Made in China 2025' plan issued," 19 May 2015. <https://english.www.gov.cn/policies/latest_releases/2015/05/19/content_281475110703534.htm>
  2. CRS IF10964, "Made in China 2025 and Industrial Policies: Issues for Congress." <https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/IF10964>
  3. USCC, "Made in China 2025: Evaluating China's Performance." <https://www.uscc.gov/research/made-china-2025-evaluating-chinas-performance>
  4. CSIS, "Made in China 2025." <https://www.csis.org/analysis/made-china-2025>

### China Standards 2035 (中国标准2035)

- **Issued**: drafted from 2018 by the Chinese Academy of Engineering and MIIT; operationalized via the State Council and CCP Central Committee "National Standardization Development Outline" (《国家标准化发展纲要》) issued October 2021. Action plans followed July 2022 and July 2024.
- **Official text (English translation)**: <https://cset.georgetown.edu/wp-content/uploads/t0406_standardization_outline_EN.pdf>
- **Status**: active.
- **Cyber-relevant pillars**:
  - Dominate global standards-setting bodies (ITU, IEC, ISO, 3GPP) for next-generation technologies — 5G/6G, IoT, AI, quantum, electric vehicles, autonomous driving, cloud.
  - Convert technical dominance into rule-making leverage so Chinese vendors are favored / interoperable by default.
- **Sectors / targets implied**: telecom equipment vendors, R&D consortia, standards bodies' membership and voting records, foreign patent portfolios.
- **Timeline pressure**: 2035 endpoint; near-term 5G/6G standardization windows through 2027.
- **Summary**: Standards 2035 is the natural follow-on to MIC2025: where MIC2025 is about making the technology, Standards 2035 is about writing the rules under which everyone else uses it. Cyber tasking under this doctrine targets standards-body internal deliberations, competitor R&D, and patent portfolios — and overlaps with Digital Silk Road exports (Huawei, ZTE, Hikvision) that bake Chinese standards into BRI partner countries.
- **Citations**:
  1. CCP CC and State Council, National Standardization Development Outline (CSET translation). <https://cset.georgetown.edu/wp-content/uploads/t0406_standardization_outline_EN.pdf>
  2. China Briefing, "The China Standards 2035 Strategy: Recent Developments." <https://www.china-briefing.com/news/china-standards-2035-strategy-recent-developments-and-their-implications-foreign-companies/>
  3. CNBC, "China Standards 2035: Behind Beijing's plan to shape future technology." <https://www.cnbc.com/2020/04/27/china-standards-2035-explained.html>

### 14th Five-Year Plan (2021–2025) — including National Informatization sub-plan

- **Issued**: National People's Congress, March 2021 (master Outline); CAC and State Council released the "14th Five-Year Plan for National Informatization" December 2021; digital-economy plan January 2022.
- **Official text**: master Outline EN at <https://cset.georgetown.edu/wp-content/uploads/t0284_14th_Five_Year_Plan_EN.pdf>; Informatization translation at <https://digichina.stanford.edu/work/translation-14th-five-year-plan-for-national-informatization-dec-2021/>
- **Status**: active, terminal year 2025.
- **Cyber-relevant pillars**:
  - "Strategic and forward-looking" S&T priorities: quantum information, integrated circuits, key software, AI, brain-inspired chips, big data, blockchain, biotech, deep-space / deep-sea / polar.
  - Build "national strategic scientific and technological strength"; close gaps in seven "chokepoint" technologies that the U.S. could cut off.
  - Critical Information Infrastructure (CII) protection regime under the Cybersecurity Law / Data Security Law.
- **Sectors / targets implied**: semiconductor toolchain (EUV, ASML), advanced packaging, EDA software, GPU/AI accelerator IP, biotech IP, quantum research.
- **Timeline pressure**: 2025 endpoint; "chokepoint" closure pressure intensified after the October 2022 U.S. export controls.
- **Summary**: The 14th FYP operationalizes MIC2025 with explicit FYP indicators and budgets. Its "chokepoint" framing — i.e., the technologies the U.S. and allies can deny — is the doctrinal language that most directly drives cyber tasking against semiconductor equipment makers, EDA vendors, AI-chip designers, and the academic / national-lab base behind them. Post-October-2022 U.S. export controls created an acute pressure window that aligns with observed Salt Typhoon, Volt Typhoon, and APT41 tempo increases.
- **Citations**:
  1. CSET translation, Outline of the 14th Five-Year Plan. <https://cset.georgetown.edu/wp-content/uploads/t0284_14th_Five_Year_Plan_EN.pdf>
  2. DigiChina, 14th Five-Year Plan for National Informatization. <https://digichina.stanford.edu/work/translation-14th-five-year-plan-for-national-informatization-dec-2021/>
  3. CRS IF11684, "China's 14th Five-Year Plan: A First Look." <https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/IF11684>

### 15th Five-Year Plan (2026–2030)

- **Issued**: approved by the NPC on 12 March 2026; CCP CC plenum communiqué October 2025.
- **Official text**: forthcoming full English release via gov.cn; commentary via IISS and DigiChina.
- **Status**: active (entered force March 2026).
- **Cyber-relevant pillars**:
  - "Modernized industrial system" elevated above pure innovation as the headline theme — i.e., productionize prior decade's R&D.
  - 109 major engineering projects covering semiconductors, embodied AI, commercial spaceflight, controlled nuclear fusion, new-type batteries, green hydrogen.
  - "AI+" action plan (released August 2025) — 90% AI penetration of devices/applications across PRC industry by 2030.
  - 6G mobile communications listed as core priority.
- **Sectors / targets implied**: AI accelerator IP, foundation-model weights and training data, fusion / battery / hydrogen research, commercial space hardware (launch, satellite buses, optical comms), 6G standards.
- **Timeline pressure**: 2030 endpoint; aligns with PLA "centenary modernization goal" (2027) and 2035 long-range objectives.
- **Summary**: The 15th FYP shifts emphasis from "discover the tech" to "produce the tech at scale." For cyber, this implies sustained pressure on industrial process knowledge — fab process recipes, fusion-engineering test data, AI training pipelines — rather than purely upstream research. The plan's explicit AI focus also creates demand for stolen model weights and large training corpora.

### Military-Civil Fusion (军民融合 / MCF)

- **Issued**: elevated to national-level strategy by Xi Jinping in 2014–2015; Central Commission for Integrated Military and Civilian Development established January 2017 (Xi chairs it personally).
- **Official text**: CCP CC documents; no single public master text. U.S. State Department archive page summarizes the doctrine: <https://2017-2021.state.gov/military-civil-fusion/>
- **Status**: active. Rebranded internally as "integrated national strategic system and capabilities" but the substance continues.
- **Cyber-relevant pillars**:
  - Erase wall between PLA and civilian industry — civilian R&D feeds military requirements and vice versa.
  - Targets: quantum, big data, semiconductors, 5G, advanced nuclear, aerospace, AI, biotech, autonomous systems.
  - Explicitly authorizes "all available means" — including cyber and traditional industrial espionage, talent programs, FDI, joint ventures, academic collaboration.
- **Sectors / targets implied**: any dual-use research; especially university labs, national labs, aerospace primes, semiconductor toolmakers, biotech firms, AI labs.
- **Timeline pressure**: continuous; aligned with PLA 2027 / 2035 modernization milestones.
- **Summary**: MCF is the meta-doctrine that licenses MSS and PLA cyber units to treat the entire foreign civilian R&D base as a military intelligence target. It explains why apparently commercial cyber theft (e.g., APT10 against pharma) sits alongside obvious military espionage (Su Bin against C-17, APT40 against naval) inside the same campaigns — both feed the same fused requirements pipeline.
- **Citations**:
  1. U.S. State Dept (archive), "The CCP's Military-Civil Fusion Policy." <https://2017-2021.state.gov/military-civil-fusion/>
  2. CNAS, "Myths and Realities of China's Military-Civil Fusion Strategy." <https://www.cnas.org/publications/reports/myths-and-realities-of-chinas-military-civil-fusion-strategy>
  3. FDD, "Defusing Military-Civil Fusion." <https://www.fdd.org/analysis/2021/05/26/defusing-military-civil-fusion/>

### Dual Circulation Strategy (双循环)

- **Issued**: Xi Jinping speech, May 2020; codified in the 14th FYP.
- **Status**: active.
- **Cyber-relevant pillars**:
  - Reduce dependence on foreign supply chains (the "internal circulation" — domestic demand + domestic supply).
  - Retain external leverage where China is indispensable (the "external circulation").
- **Sectors / targets implied**: any foreign technology that the U.S. or allies could choke off — semiconductors, EDA software, aerospace engines, advanced agriculture, biotech reagents.
- **Timeline pressure**: continuous; intensified by U.S. export controls (Oct 2022 onward).
- **Summary**: Dual Circulation is the macro-strategic frame that gives MIC2025 / 14th FYP / MCF their urgency. Cyber operations against U.S. and allied chokepoint technology providers can be linked here when the strategic-goal framing is broader than a single sector — e.g., bulk theft of EDA / IP-block libraries serves "internal circulation" in semiconductors, not any one device.
- **Citations**:
  1. CSIS, "Made in China 2025" (situates Dual Circulation alongside MIC2025).
  2. DigiChina commentary on 14th FYP framing.

### Belt and Road Initiative / Digital Silk Road (一带一路 / 数字丝绸之路)

- **Issued**: BRI announced by Xi Jinping, 2013 (Astana / Jakarta speeches); Digital Silk Road formalized in March 2015 "Vision and Actions" white paper from NDRC, MOFA, MOFCOM. CAC reported 17 DSR-specific MOUs by 2021.
- **Status**: active.
- **Cyber-relevant pillars**:
  - Export Chinese telecom, fiber, undersea cable, data-center, satellite, and surveillance infrastructure to BRI partner countries.
  - Embed Chinese standards (linked to Standards 2035) into partner-country regulation.
  - Cultivate political leverage and signals-intelligence access via deployed infrastructure.
- **Sectors / targets implied**: BRI partner-country telecom operators, ministries that procure DSR infrastructure, BRI project competitors, Western alternatives (Open RAN, BDN).
- **Timeline pressure**: continuous.
- **Summary**: DSR is both a market-expansion doctrine and a counterintelligence-relevant footprint expansion. APT40's explicit Hainan MSS tasking against "industries included in China's Belt and Road Initiative" (per the 2021 CISA advisory) is the cleanest attested linkage. Mustang Panda's operations against Southeast Asian governments often align with BRI partner-country political reporting requirements.
- **Citations**:
  1. CFR, "China's Digital Silk Road Initiative." <https://www.cfr.org/china-digital-silk-road/>
  2. CISA AA21-200a (cites APT40 BRI tasking). <https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/cybersecurity-advisories/aa21-200a>
  3. CSIS Reconnecting Asia, "China Doubles Down on Its Digital Silk Road." <https://reconasia.csis.org/china-doubles-down-its-digital-silk-road/>

### 2017 National Intelligence Law (国家情报法)

- **Issued**: NPC Standing Committee, 27 June 2017; amended 27 April 2018.
- **Official text**: <https://www.chinalawtranslate.com/en/national-intelligence-law-of-the-p-r-c-2017/>
- **Status**: active.
- **Cyber-relevant pillars**:
  - Article 7: "All organizations and citizens shall support, assist, and cooperate with national intelligence efforts in accordance with law, and shall protect national intelligence work secrets they are aware of."
  - Article 14: empowers intelligence organs to compel cooperation from individuals and entities.
  - Combined with the 2014 Counter-Espionage Law, the 2015 National Security Law, and the 2017 Cybersecurity Law, creates a legal regime under which any PRC-domiciled firm can be compelled to provide data or access.
- **Sectors / targets implied**: not a tasking doctrine per se; rather the legal supply-side enabler that converts every Chinese tech firm (Huawei, Tencent, Alibaba, TikTok, DJI, Hikvision) into a potential intelligence collection node.
- **Timeline pressure**: continuous.
- **Summary**: The Intelligence Law is the legal mechanism behind every concern about Chinese-vendor equipment in Western critical infrastructure. It's the doctrinal counterpart to the Digital Silk Road export footprint: DSR places the gear, the Intelligence Law obligates the vendor to cooperate with MSS / MPS / PLA if compelled. Cited heavily in U.S. and allied export-restriction and procurement-ban rationales.
- **Citations**:
  1. China Law Translate, EN text of the National Intelligence Law. <https://www.chinalawtranslate.com/en/national-intelligence-law-of-the-p-r-c-2017/>
  2. Lawfare, "Beijing's New National Intelligence Law: From Defense to Offense." <https://www.lawfaremedia.org/article/beijings-new-national-intelligence-law-defense-offense>
  3. The Diplomat, "The Real Danger of China's National Intelligence Law." <https://thediplomat.com/2019/02/the-real-danger-of-chinas-national-intelligence-law/>

### Data Security Law (DSL) and Personal Information Protection Law (PIPL)

- **Issued**: DSL by NPC, June 2021 (effective 1 Sep 2021); PIPL by NPC, August 2021 (effective 1 Nov 2021). Together with the 2017 Cybersecurity Law (CSL) they form the "three pillars."
- **Status**: active.
- **Cyber-relevant pillars**:
  - Data localization for critical information infrastructure operators (CIIOs) and large processors.
  - Mandatory security review for outbound transfer of "important data" or large volumes of personal data.
  - State asserts sovereign control over data classified as relevant to national security.
- **Sectors / targets implied**: not an offensive doctrine, but the defensive counterpart that shapes (a) what foreign firms must store inside China (creating MSS access opportunities) and (b) what the PRC views as legitimate to seize from PRC-based foreign businesses.
- **Timeline pressure**: continuous; tightened by CAC rules in 2024.
- **Summary**: DSL/PIPL are normally treated as compliance doctrine, but for cyber-strategic analysis they matter because they cement the "data sovereignty" frame that justifies CAC oversight of cross-border flows and creates the legal basis for MSS access to data held inside China by foreign firms.
- **Citations**:
  1. DLA Piper, "Data protection laws in China." <https://www.dlapiperdataprotection.com/index.html?c=CN>
  2. ICAS, "China's Data Governance and Cybersecurity Regime." <https://chinaus-icas.org/research/chinas-data-governance-and-cybersecurity-regime/>

### "Informatized" and "Intelligentized" Warfare (信息化战争 / 智能化战争)

- **Issued**: "Informatized warfare" — PLA doctrine published from 2004 ("Science of Military Strategy"). "Intelligentized warfare" — articulated in 2019 PLA white paper "China's National Defense in the New Era" and elaborated in the 2020 "Science of Military Strategy."
- **Official text**: 2019 PLA Defense White Paper (English at <http://english.www.gov.cn/archive/whitepaper/201907/24/content_WS5d3941ddc6d08408f502283d.html>).
- **Status**: active. April 2024: PLA Strategic Support Force (SSF, est. 2015) dissolved into three new "arms" — Aerospace Force, Cyberspace Force, Information Support Force — reporting directly to the Central Military Commission. This reorganization is widely read as the operational instantiation of "intelligentized" warfare.
- **Cyber-relevant pillars**:
  - "System destruction warfare" (体系破击战): defeat enemy by paralyzing C4ISR nodes rather than attriting forces.
  - Cyber, space, EW, psychological ops treated as integrated "strategic" domains.
  - AI-enabled command and control; algorithmic decision support.
- **Sectors / targets implied**: U.S. INDOPACOM logistics, Guam infrastructure, undersea cable choke points, satellite C2 ground stations, allied military command networks, civilian critical infrastructure that supports military mobilization (ports, fuel, rail, electricity, water, telecom).
- **Timeline pressure**: PLA 2027 centenary milestone; intelligentized force build-out through 2035; "fully modernized" by 2049.
- **Summary**: This is the doctrine behind Volt Typhoon and Salt Typhoon. "System destruction warfare" tells PLA planners that the highest-leverage cyber effects in a Taiwan contingency are against the U.S. ability to project force across the Pacific — not against the Taiwanese military directly. Pre-positioning in Guam telecom, U.S. West Coast water/electric utilities, and pipeline operators is the operational expression of this doctrine.
- **Citations**:
  1. PLA Defense White Paper, "China's National Defense in the New Era" (2019). <http://english.www.gov.cn/archive/whitepaper/201907/24/content_WS5d3941ddc6d08408f502283d.html>
  2. NDU / John Costello & Joe McReynolds, "China's Strategic Support Force: A Force for a New Era." <https://digitalcommons.ndu.edu/china-strategic-perspectives/6/>
  3. Brookings, "The PLA's Strategic Support Force and AI Innovation." <https://www.brookings.edu/articles/the-plas-strategic-support-force-and-ai-innovation-china-military-tech/>
  4. CNA, "The Chinese Military's New Information Support Force." <https://www.cna.org/our-media/indepth/2024/08/chinese-information-support-force>
  5. Defense One, "The future of China's new information support force." <https://www.defenseone.com/ideas/2025/02/future-chinas-new-information-support-force/402677/>

### Taiwan Reunification ("One China") and the "Davidson Window" framing

- **Issued (PRC side)**: continuous since 1949; codified in 2005 Anti-Secession Law; PLA 2027 modernization milestone announced by Xi 2017 ("comprehensively improve strategic capabilities … by 2027"). "Davidson Window" is a U.S. analytic frame, not a PRC doctrine — named after Adm. Phil Davidson's March 2021 INDOPACOM testimony estimating the period 2021–2027 as the highest-risk window.
- **Status**: active. U.S. IC assessment as of 2026 is that PRC leadership has not committed to a fixed invasion date but is materially preparing for the option.
- **Cyber-relevant pillars (PRC)**:
  - "Reunification" treated as a sovereignty-and-territorial-integrity core interest; PLA prepared to use force if peaceful options are exhausted.
  - Cyber tasking pre-conflict: collection on Taiwan government, defense, semiconductors (TSMC), telecom, electricity, undersea cables; influence operations / cognitive domain ops.
  - Cyber tasking on U.S. and allies pre-conflict: pre-positioning for disruption of force projection (Volt Typhoon); collection on U.S. decision-makers (Storm-0558, Salt Typhoon).
- **Sectors / targets implied**: Taiwan government and military networks; Taiwan semiconductor and energy infrastructure; U.S. Indo-Pacific logistics; U.S./allied telecom carrying military comms; Japanese / Filipino / Korean basing-relevant infrastructure.
- **Timeline pressure**: PLA 2027 milestone (most-cited).
- **Summary**: The Taiwan contingency is the single most-cited rationale by U.S. and allied attributing authorities for the post-2021 surge in pre-positioning operations. The CISA / NSA / FBI Volt Typhoon advisory of February 2024 (AA24-038a) explicitly assesses that the pre-positioning is "to enable lateral movement to OT assets to disrupt functions" and is "in the event of a major crisis or conflict with the United States" — a near-paraphrase of Taiwan-contingency scenario language.
- **Citations**:
  1. CISA AA24-038a (assessment of pre-positioning purpose). <https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/cybersecurity-advisories/aa24-038a>
  2. Lowy Institute, "China, Taiwan, and the PLA's 2027 milestones." <https://www.lowyinstitute.org/the-interpreter/china-taiwan-pla-s-2027-milestones>
  3. Wikipedia / synthesis, "Davidson window." <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Davidson_window>
  4. USNI News, "China Not Committed to 2027 Taiwan Invasion, U.S. Intel Report Says" (March 2026 ODNI assessment). <https://news.usni.org/2026/03/19/china-not-committed-to-2027-taiwan-invasion-u-s-intel-report-says>
