# Iran — Cyber-relevant Strategic Doctrines

### Forward Defense ("Defa-e Pishdastane") / Mosaic Defense

- **Issued**: evolved continuously since the Iran–Iraq war; the "forward" layer formally elaborated c. 2012 by senior IRGC commanders (Qasem Soleimani, Mohammad Bagheri); reaffirmed throughout Khamenei's tenure.
- **Official text**: no single doctrinal document; reconstructed from Supreme Leader speeches at IRGC events and senior commander interviews on Tasnim/Fars; analysed by IRAM Center and the Jerusalem Institute for Strategy and Security. [1][2]
- **Status**: active (the dominant Iranian military framework).
- **Cyber-relevant pillars**:
  - Engage adversaries outside the homeland through proxies and asymmetric means.
  - Maintain decentralised, deniable capabilities resilient to leadership decapitation ("mosaic" of independent cells).
  - Treat cyber, drones, ballistic missiles, and maritime swarming as one integrated asymmetric portfolio.
- **Sectors / targets implied**: Israeli civil and military infrastructure; US bases and contractors in the region; Gulf energy infrastructure; Western interests in Iraq/Syria/Lebanon/Yemen.
- **Timeline pressure**: continuous; intensifies during nuclear-file confrontations and after kinetic Israeli strikes (e.g. April 2024 Damascus consulate strike, June 2025 strikes on Natanz/Fordow).
- **Summary** (~150 words): Forward Defense holds that Iran's geographic and conventional inferiority versus Israel and the United States requires fighting at maximum range, through partners and proxies, so that conflict never reaches Iranian soil. The "mosaic" element — drawn from Iran–Iraq war doctrine — adds that capability must be distributed so that no single decapitation strike can disable it. Cyber operations fit this doctrine cleanly: they project Iranian power into adversary networks at near-zero physical risk, exploit deniability via hacktivist personas and contractor fronts, and survive when individual operators are exposed. Every IRGC and MOIS cyber operation against Israeli or US critical infrastructure can be read as the cyber expression of forward defense.

  2. Jerusalem Institute for Strategy and Security, "Iran's Mosaic Defense Faces Its Real Test", https://jiss.org.il/en/grinberg-irans-mosaic-defense-faces-its-real-test/

### Axis of Resistance ("Mehvar-e Moghavemat")

- **Issued**: term in continuous regime usage since the late 1990s; institutionalised under Qasem Soleimani as Quds Force commander (1998–2020).
- **Official text**: regime framing on khamenei.ir and Supreme Leader speeches; MFA and Tasnim/Fars references; no consolidated written charter.
- **Status**: active (re-affirmed by President Pezeshkian and Foreign Minister Araghchi in 2024-25 statements).
- **Cyber-relevant pillars**:
  - Networked deterrence: Hezbollah, Houthis, Iraqi PMF, Hamas/PIJ, Syrian remnants, and aligned actors.
  - Capability transfer downward (drones, missiles, also cyber tooling to Hezbollah's cyber unit and Houthi cyber affiliates).
  - Information-warfare component: pro-Resistance influence operations across Arabic, English, Persian, Spanish, French media.
- **Sectors / targets implied**: Israel and any actor supporting Israel; Gulf monarchies; US presence in Iraq/Syria/Persian Gulf.
- **Timeline pressure**: severely stressed by 2023-25 degradation of Hezbollah/Hamas; cyber may be the rebuilding axis.
- **Summary** (~150 words): The Axis of Resistance is Iran's regional alliance structure built around shared anti-Israel, anti-US, anti-Saudi posture. After the kinetic degradation of Hezbollah and Hamas in 2023-25 and the fall of the Assad regime, cyber capability transfer and joint cyber tasking against Israel and US interests have plausibly grown in relative importance. Lebanese Cedar (also known as Volatile Cedar) is the documented Hezbollah cyber capability; Check Point's 2015 and 2021 reports plus later analysis indicate IRGC-Quds Force capacity-building of Hezbollah's cyber arm. Iranian-flag hacktivist personas frequently coordinate Telegram amplification with Axis-aligned channels.

### Asymmetric Warfare as Response to Conventional Inferiority

- **Issued**: doctrinal foundation since the 1980s; formalised in IRGC strategic writings during the 2000s.
- **Official text**: not centralised; reflected in IRGC Navy doctrine, drone-program speeches by Hossein Salami and Amir Ali Hajizadeh, and Khamenei's repeated rejection of negotiating from weakness.
- **Status**: active.
- **Cyber-relevant pillars**:
  - Accept conventional inferiority versus US/Israel; close the gap with deniable, cheap, scalable tools.
  - Cyber operations chosen specifically because they are low-cost, attributable but not provably state-directed, and difficult to retaliate against in kind.
  - Tolerance for collateral damage to civilian infrastructure (water, finance, healthcare) when targeted asymmetrically against adversary civilians.
- **Sectors / targets implied**: any soft-target sector in adversary states; OT/SCADA; financial systems; influence ops.
- **Timeline pressure**: aligned with periods of conventional pressure (Soleimani killing, Israeli campaigns, 2025 US strikes).
- **Summary** (~150 words): Iran's recognition that direct military confrontation with the US or Israel is unwinnable has driven a forty-year investment in asymmetric capabilities. Cyber sits at the apex of this portfolio because of its cost structure, deniability, and global reach. Senior IRGC commanders have publicly described cyber as a domain where Iran can punch above its weight; this is the doctrinal logic behind targeting Israeli water utilities (April 2020), US dams (Bowman 2013), and US financial DDoS (Ababil 2012-13).

### Constitutional Role of the IRGC (Article 150)

- **Issued**: 1979 Constitution of the Islamic Republic, Article 150; expanded by 1992 IRGC Law.
- **Official text**: Constitution of the Islamic Republic of Iran, https://irandataportal.syr.edu/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Constitution.pdf
- **Status**: active; foundational.
- **Cyber-relevant pillars**:
  - Mandates the IRGC to "safeguard the Revolution and its achievements" — interpreted broadly to authorise extraterritorial action.
  - Authorises a parallel intelligence apparatus (IRGC-IO) separate from MOIS, with explicit counter-dissident and counter-foreign-threat mission.
  - Provides legal cover for IRGC commercial entities (Khatam al-Anbiya) and contractor fronts that perform cyber tasking.
- **Sectors / targets implied**: any actor or sector judged hostile to the Revolution.
- **Timeline pressure**: none; permanent constitutional authority.
- **Summary** (~150 words): The constitutional role of the IRGC is the doctrinal source code for everything from extraterritorial assassination to credential-phishing journalists who criticise the Supreme Leader. The dual-intelligence structure (IRGC-IO alongside MOIS) deliberately creates competing services, which is why we see parallel actor clusters (CyberAv3ngers under IRGC-IO; HomeLand Justice under MOIS) running similar destructive-leak operations.
- **Citations**:
  1. Constitution of Iran, Article 150; English text via Syracuse Iran Data Portal.
  2. CFR, "The Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps", https://www.cfr.org/backgrounders/irans-revolutionary-guards

### Sanctions Evasion as State Policy ("Resistance Economy" / "Knowledge-Based Economy" / "Surge in Production")

- **Issued**: framework articulated by Khamenei from 2014 onward as "Resistance Economy"; reinforced by every annual Nowruz "Year of …" designation from 2018 to 2025.
  - 1402 (Mar 2023 – Mar 2024): "Inflation Control, Growth in Production"
  - 1403 (Mar 2024 – Mar 2025): "Surge in Production Through People's Participation"
- **Official text**: Khamenei Nowruz addresses on english.khamenei.ir and leader.ir.
- **Status**: active; foundational to economic strategy.
- **Cyber-relevant pillars**:
  - Cyber as a means of sanctions evasion (crypto laundering, sanctioned-entity payment rails, oil-revenue movement).
  - IP theft from Western industries with import-substitution potential (aerospace, petrochemicals, semiconductors).
  - Revenue generation through ransomware (Pay2Key, Najee Technology/Afkar System) to fund regime and IRGC operations.
- **Sectors / targets implied**: financial institutions, crypto exchanges, energy and dual-use IP holders.
- **Timeline pressure**: maximum-pressure sanctions reinstated Feb 2025 (NSPM-2); snapback in 2025.
- **Summary** (~150 words): Khamenei's annual designation of economic priorities and his "Resistance Economy" frame mean that sanctions evasion is not opportunistic but doctrinal. Treasury OFAC has tracked record Iranian crypto flows ($7.8B in 2025 per Chainalysis-tracked Iranian wallets) and designated IRGC-linked exchanges (Zedcex / Zedxion in Jan 2026). Cyber operations that look financial (Pay2Key, Pioneer Kitten ransomware affiliate work) are simultaneously sanctions-evasion revenue and asymmetric harassment — the regime accepts no clean separation.

### Post-Soleimani Retaliation Posture

- **Issued**: implicit since the January 3, 2020 US strike on Qasem Soleimani; explicit in Khamenei and IRGC commander speeches from 2020 onward.
- **Official text**: Khamenei statements on khamenei.ir; Soleimani-Foundation amplification via Tasnim.
- **Status**: active. Codified by DOJ in its September 2024 indictment of three IRGC officers, which states the activity "advance[s] the malign activities of the IRGC, including ongoing efforts to avenge the death of Qasem Soleimani". [1]
- **Cyber-relevant pillars**:
  - Targeting of former Trump-administration officials, military officers, and IRGC adversaries (Pompeo, Brian Hook, John Bolton, Esper).
  - Targeting of US political figures and campaign infrastructure as part of "discord and revenge" framework.
  - Persistent credential-phishing against journalists who reported on Soleimani.
- **Sectors / targets implied**: US political campaigns, current/former officials, defence consultants, media, think-tanks.
- **Timeline pressure**: continuous; anniversary periods (early January) see surges.
- **Summary** (~150 words): The killing of Soleimani created an explicit revenge obligation that the regime publicly maintains. The 2024 DOJ indictment of Jalili, Aghamiri, and Balaghi formally ties APT42-style hack-and-leak operations to Soleimani retaliation — making this one of the few cyber doctrines where the US government has stipulated the strategic motive in court, lifting most events linked here to "attested" confidence.
- **Citations**:
  1. DOJ, "Three IRGC Cyber Actors Indicted for 'Hack-and-Leak' Operation Designed to Influence the 2024 U.S. Presidential Election", https://www.justice.gov/archives/opa/pr/three-irgc-cyber-actors-indicted-hack-and-leak-operation-designed-influence-2024-us

### Cyber as Deniable Retaliation via Hacktivist / Proxy Fronts

- **Issued**: inferred operational doctrine; formalised in pattern of personas from 2020 onward.
- **Official text**: no published Iranian doctrine; the doctrine is reconstructed from Western government attribution that repeatedly identifies state services behind hacktivist masks.
- **Status**: inferred, but heavily attested via Treasury and CISA documents.
- **Cyber-relevant pillars**:
  - Conduct destructive or coercive operations behind a hacktivist persona to preserve plausible deniability.
  - Use Telegram and X channels for claim-and-leak operations.
  - Match each adversary to a persona: HomeLand Justice for MEK/Albania, CyberAv3ngers for Israel-equipment retaliation, Moses Staff for direct anti-Israel coercion, Black Shadow for Israeli leaks, Emennet Pasargad for US election interference.
- **Sectors / targets implied**: any sector aligned to an active diplomatic or kinetic confrontation.
- **Timeline pressure**: synchronised to political/military events (Iran-Israel exchanges, MEK conferences, US elections).
- **Summary** (~150 words): The hacktivist-front doctrine is the operational pattern that makes everything else work. By laundering IRGC-IO or MOIS operations through a named persona that posts on Telegram, the regime can attack civilian infrastructure (water, hospitals, government services) while denying state direction. US Treasury and CISA have pierced this veil at least four times: HomeLand Justice → MOIS (Sept 2022); CyberAv3ngers → IRGC-IO (Dec 2023 / Feb 2024); Emennet Pasargad's "Enemies of the People" → IRGC contractor (Nov 2021); DEV-0270 / Nemesis Kitten → Phosphorus subgroup (Microsoft 2022).
