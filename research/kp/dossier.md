# DPRK cyber dossier

> Canonical AUSPEX synthesis for the Democratic People's Republic of Korea (DPRK). Cross-reference: `doctrines.md`, `actors.md`, `events.md`, `sources.md`.

## Executive summary

The DPRK runs the only state cyber program in the world where the dominant strategic goal is **regime hard-currency revenue generation in direct support of WMD/missile development**, with espionage and disruption operating as secondary lines. Treasury, FBI, CISA, and (until April 2024) the UN Panel of Experts have repeatedly and explicitly tied DPRK cyber-theft proceeds — most prominently from cryptocurrency exchanges and DeFi bridges — to the funding of Pyongyang's nuclear and ballistic-missile programs. That linkage is the "attested" gold standard under the AUSPEX schema: the attributing government names the doctrinal goal in the sanctioning/indictment document itself.

Cumulative DPRK crypto theft on public record now exceeds **$6 billion** (Chainalysis, Dec 2025), including a single-incident record of **$1.5B from Bybit in Feb 2025** (FBI attribution, IC3 PSA250226). 2024 alone: ~$1.34B across 47 incidents; 2025: $2.0B+. The FBI publicly attributes these operations to the **TraderTraitor** subset of Lazarus, which the FBI explicitly maps to the **3rd Bureau of the RGB** (Reconnaissance General Bureau).

## Top doctrines (full text in `doctrines.md`)

1. **Juche / self-reliance** — the foundational ideology since Kim Il Sung, providing the political logic for indigenous offensive cyber as a low-cost asymmetric capability.
2. **Byungjin (parallel nuclear + economic development)** — formally declared complete in April 2018 once nuclear "force" was deemed achieved; the doctrinal predecessor under which cyber-theft scaled.
3. **Post-2018 nuclear-force modernization line / 8th Party Congress Five-Year Defense Plan (2021–2025)** — Kim Jong Un's published roadmap for 13 new strategic systems (tactical nukes, MIRVs, hypersonics, solid-fuel ICBMs, recon satellites, nuclear-powered SSBN, underwater drones). This is the **current driver of cyber tasking**: every cryptocurrency heist and every IT-worker placement directly subsidizes line items on this plan.
4. **Songun (military-first)** — Kim Jong Il-era ideology; formally de-emphasized by Kim Jong Un in 2021 in favor of "people-first politics," but the institutional infrastructure of the military-intel cyber units it built (RGB, Bureau 121, Lab 110) is fully intact.
5. **Sanctions-evasion-as-state-policy** — inferred/operationalized through Bureau 39 (Office 39) of the WPK Central Committee, the regime's hard-currency slush-fund apparatus that has progressively absorbed cyber as its #1 revenue channel.
6. **Cyber-as-asymmetric-force-generation / hard-currency mechanism** — strongly inferred from Treasury, UN PoE, and Mandiant reporting; never published as a formal doctrine, but is the operative principle that ties RGB cyber tasking to KPA modernization.

## Top services

- **Reconnaissance General Bureau (RGB)** — DPRK's primary foreign intelligence and clandestine-operations service, established in 2009. Owner of the cyber portfolio.
  - **Bureau 121** — historical hacker unit name; in practice Mandiant assesses this is now **Lab 110**, an expanded/reorganized version. Front-company cover historically out of Dalian (Chosun Expo Joint Venture) and Shenyang.
  - **3rd Bureau (RGB)** — explicitly identified by FBI in the Bybit PSA as the parent of the TraderTraitor financial-theft cluster.
  - **Lab 110** — umbrella for TEMP.Hermit (Lazarus core), APT38, and Andariel per Mandiant (March 2024, "Not So Lazarus").
- **Ministry of State Security (MSS)** — domestic counterintel and external counterintel. APT37 / ScarCruft is attributed to MSS by multiple vendors, distinguishing it operationally from RGB.
- **Workers' Party of Korea Central Committee Bureau 39 (Office 39)** — non-cyber service per se, but the receiver-of-record for hard currency generated by cyber theft and IT-worker fraud; the financial backbone of regime sanctions evasion.
- **United Front Department (UFD)** — believed to task some Kimsuky operations against the diaspora, defectors, and policy targets.

## Target sector pattern

The DPRK's targeting profile is **distinctively bifurcated**:
- **Money-extraction track**: cryptocurrency exchanges, DeFi bridges, crypto custody/wallet vendors, Web3 employees as initial-access vectors, online gambling/casino platforms, SWIFT-network smaller-tier banks (still observed pre-2020), recently expanded to **supply-chain pivots** (3CX, JumpCloud, Safe{Wallet}/Bybit, npm/PyPI ecosystem) where the supplier is collateral and the downstream crypto customer is the target.
- **Strategic-intel track**: ROK/US/JP foreign-policy think tanks, nuclear policy researchers, diaspora and defector communities, journalists covering DPRK, ROK & US defense contractors (aerospace, missile defense, naval), NASA OIG (cf. Andariel/Rim Jong Hyok indictment 2024), healthcare and pharma (dual purpose — espionage + ransomware revenue).

Notably absent from DPRK tasking compared to RU/CN/IR: critical-infrastructure pre-positioning against the US homeland, election influence operations, and broad consumer-data brokerage. DPRK cyber is mission-focused and revenue-driven.

## Signature events (selected; full list in `events.md`)

- **2013-03 DarkSeoul** — wipers against 3 ROK broadcasters + 3 banks; first major destructive DPRK op publicly attributed.
- **2014-11 Sony Pictures** — destructive attack tied to *The Interview*; FBI public attribution, basis of 2018 Park Jin Hyok indictment.
- **2016-02 Bangladesh Bank SWIFT heist** — $81M out, $850M attempted; APT38 origin event.
- **2017-05 WannaCry** — global ransomware worm; UK, US, AU, CA, NZ, JP joint attribution to DPRK.
- **2022-03 Ronin / Axie Infinity** — $625M, largest DeFi hack at the time; Treasury sanctioned the receiving ETH wallet — first DPRK crypto-wallet OFAC SDN.
- **2022-06 Harmony Horizon Bridge** — $100M; FBI public attribution Jan 2023.
- **2023-03 3CX supply-chain** — first publicly documented software-supply-chain attack with cascading downstream supply-chain origin (X_TRADER → 3CX).
- **2023-06 Atomic Wallet** — $100M (Elliptic).
- **2023-07 JumpCloud** — supply-chain pivot used to hit ~5 downstream crypto firms.
- **2024-05 DMM Bitcoin** — $308M; FBI/NPA/DC3 joint attribution to TraderTraitor.
- **2024-07 Andariel indictment** (Rim Jong Hyok) — DOJ formally names hospital ransomware as proceeds-feeder to defense-contractor and NASA intrusions.
- **2024-08 Citrine Sleet Chromium zero-day** (CVE-2024-7971) — FudModule rootkit, crypto-focused targeting.
- **2025-02 Bybit / TraderTraitor** — $1.5B, largest crypto heist in history.

## Linkage-confidence posture

- **Attested**: any event where Treasury, FBI, DOJ or CISA names the strategic goal (WMD/missile financing) in the public document. Apply to all post-2020 crypto-theft incidents per OFAC's standardized "support DPRK WMD program" language.
- **Strongly inferred**: defense-contractor and aerospace intrusions by Andariel (the 2024 Rim Jong Hyok indictment links proceeds to "additional computer intrusions into defense, technology, and government entities worldwide" — defense targeting itself is doctrine-aligned even where Treasury doesn't explicitly name it).
- **Plausible**: APT37/MSS surveillance ops on individuals where no published linkage to a specific doctrinal pillar exists — assume Juche-internal-security tasking by default.

## Gaps and watchlist

1. **Post-UN-PoE blackout**: independent quantification of DPRK sanctions evasion is now weaker. MSMT publications are the new baseline; AUSPEX should ingest them as they release.
2. **Russia-DPRK cyber cooperation**: under-attested. The 2023 Kim-Putin summit and subsequent arms-transfer documentation raises a doctrinal question of whether DPRK cyber is being lent to Russian targeting needs. No public attribution as of this dossier.
3. **PRC facilitation**: most DPRK cyber operators remain physically based in NE China, and laundering routinely transits PRC OTC desks. The PRC-as-enabler dimension is doctrinally important but politically under-named in US/ROK attribution documents.
4. **DPRK IT-worker insider-threat pivot**: in 2024–2025 reporting (Treasury, Microsoft Jasper Sleet) the IT-worker scheme is increasingly observed *demanding extortion payments after termination* and exfiltrating proprietary data — this is a doctrinal evolution from passive wage-fraud to active insider espionage.
