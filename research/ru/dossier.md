# Russia — Cyber Program Dossier

## Executive Summary

Russia's state-sponsored cyber program is the most operationally productive and most kinetically integrated in the world. Where China's offensive cyber serves a long-arc industrial-policy and reunification doctrine and Iran's serves regime survival and proxy-warfare, Russia's serves a contiguous, gray-zone "information confrontation" doctrine that treats cyber, influence, and conventional force as a single continuum. Three intelligence services — GRU (military), FSB (security), SVR (foreign) — operate distinct portfolios with overlapping target sets, supplemented since 2022 by an expanded layer of criminal and "hacktivist" proxies that the US Department of Justice and Treasury OFAC have formally attributed to GRU direction.

The strategic context after February 2022 has hardened. The 2023 Foreign Policy Concept frames Russia as a civilization-state in existential contest with an "Anglo-Saxon" West; the 2021 National Security Strategy explicitly authorizes "forces and means of information confrontation"; and the December 2021 ultimatum to NATO codified non-enlargement and rollback to 1997 boundaries as the strategic war aim that cyber operations are tasked to support.

## Top Doctrines (cyber-relevant)

1. **2023 Foreign Policy Concept** — formal text; civilizational frame; cyber sovereignty plus opposition to Western "weaponization" of cyberspace.
2. **2021 National Security Strategy** — first time information security is its own section; authorizes building offensive capability.
3. **2016 Information Security Doctrine** — defines information sphere as a national-security domain on par with land/sea/air/space.
4. **2014 Military Doctrine (with subsequent implementing acts)** — codifies non-contact warfare and information confrontation under General Staff purview; names NATO enlargement as the principal threat.
5. **Gerasimov framework (informal, 2013)** — operational description of integrated hybrid warfare; the most-cited Russian-authored articulation, though not a formal doctrine.
6. **Russkiy Mir / Near Abroad** — civilizational justification for continuous operations across post-Soviet space; explains the Ukrainian theater's permanent priority.
7. **December 2021 NATO Ultimatum** — codifies NATO non-enlargement as casus belli; provides doctrinal license for operations against new accession states (Finland, Sweden) and Ukraine support infrastructure.
8. **Energy weaponization (pattern, not formal doctrine)** — pipeline and grid operations as coercive lever paired with cyber pre-positioning in target-country energy infrastructure.
9. **Post-2022 sanctions-response posture (informal)** — mobilization of criminal proxies; performative disruptive operations against Western critical infrastructure.

Confidence note: the first four are published primary texts; the rest are operational patterns reconstructed from official speeches, indictments, and observed tasking.

## Top Services

- **GRU (Glavnoye Razvedyvatelnoye Upravlenie)** — the operationally dominant service. Houses Sandworm (Unit 74455 — destructive/disruptive ops), Fancy Bear (Unit 26165 — political and military espionage, election interference), Unit 54777 (psychological operations), and assessed direction of Cyber Army of Russia Reborn. Six Unit 74455 officers indicted Oct 2020; twelve Unit 26165/74455 officers indicted Jul 2018 (Mueller); Treasury OFAC sanctions on GTsST. The destructive operations against Ukraine since 2014 are overwhelmingly GRU.
- **FSB (Federalnaya Sluzhba Bezopasnosti)** — strategic espionage at scale via Center 16 (SIGINT — Turla/Snake, Berserk Bear/Dragonfly) and Center 18 (information security — Gamaredon, Star Blizzard, ContiLeaks-implicated direction of cybercriminal groups). FSB also runs the Yahoo-era and ongoing strategic collection. Operation MEDUSA (May 2023) dismantled the FSB's flagship Snake malware infrastructure.
- **SVR (Sluzhba Vneshney Razvedki)** — pure strategic foreign intelligence collection via APT29 / Midnight Blizzard. The SolarWinds supply-chain operation (Dec 2020), the Microsoft corporate breach (Nov 2023–Jan 2024), and continuous cloud-tenant collection against US/UK/EU foreign-policy targets are the defining operations. Notably long-dwell, technically sophisticated, and rarely destructive.
- **TsNIIKhM (MoD Research Institute)** — Triton SIS-targeting capability against Saudi Petro Rabigh (Aug 2017); Treasury-sanctioned Oct 2020; Gladkikh indicted Mar 2022. Holds the most-dangerous publicly attributed Russian ICS capability.
- **Criminal and hacktivist proxies** — Conti/TrickBot ecosystem (with documented FSB liaison per ContiLeaks 2022), Killnet, NoName057(16), Cyber Army of Russia Reborn (GRU-directed per Treasury Jul 2024), XakNet. Post-2022 sanctions-response surge.

3. **APT28 / Fancy Bear (GRU Unit 26165)** — election interference, hack-and-leak, defense-sector espionage.
4. **Turla / Secret Blizzard (FSB Center 16)** — long-dwell NATO MFA and defense espionage; Snake malware lineage.
5. **Berserk Bear / Ghost Blizzard (FSB Center 16)** — energy-sector ICS pre-positioning.
6. **Gamaredon / Aqua Blizzard (FSB Center 18)** — high-volume continuous Ukrainian government targeting.
7. **Star Blizzard / Callisto (FSB Center 18)** — credential phishing of Western policy elites.
8. **Cadet Blizzard / Ember Bear (GRU)** — Ukraine-focused disruption (WhisperGate).
9. **CARR / NoName057(16) / Killnet** — performative DDoS and ICS-HMI manipulation proxies.

## Target Sector Pattern

Russia's targeting maps cleanly to doctrine. By volume and impact:

- **Critical national infrastructure of adversaries** — electric grids (Ukraine 2015, 2016, 2022, 2023, 2024 Lviv heating), satellite communications (Viasat KA-SAT 2022), telecoms (Kyivstar 2023–24), water utilities (US, 2024). Doctrine: energy weaponization, non-contact warfare, performative deterrence.
- **Government and foreign-affairs** — Bundestag 2015, Macron campaign 2017, US executive-branch agencies via SolarWinds 2020, German SPD 2024, Norwegian Storting 2020, ongoing MFA targeting across NATO. Doctrine: 2016/2023 information security/foreign policy concepts.
- **Defense industrial base and supply chain** — SolarWinds, TeamCity, JetBrains, HPE, Microsoft, defense contractors. Doctrine: SVR strategic collection.
- **Election infrastructure and political processes** — DNC 2016, French 2017, UK trade documents 2019, Romanian 2024, US state systems. Doctrine: 2014 Military Doctrine (destabilize adversary politics), information confrontation.
- **Sport, anti-doping, and prestige institutions** — WADA, IAAF, IOC, OPCW. Doctrine: regime-prestige defense.
- **Energy sector (pre-positioning)** — Berserk Bear US/UK/EU campaigns 2012–18 and ongoing; Triton/Saudi Petro Rabigh 2017.
- **Sanctions-evasion-relevant chokepoints / financial sector** — Conti/TrickBot-era healthcare and government ransomware.

## Signature Events (canonical attributions)

- **2007 Estonia** — first state-level cyber crisis; established political-cyber linkage in Western policy thinking.
- **2008 Georgia** — first cyber-kinetic integration.
- **2015 & 2016 Ukraine power grid (Sandworm)** — first acknowledged cyber-induced grid outages.
- **2016 DNC + DCleaks (GRU 26165/74455, Mueller indictment)** — defining election-interference operation.
- **2017 NotPetya (Sandworm)** — "most destructive and costly cyberattack in history" per White House; $10B global damage.
- **2017 Triton (TsNIIKhM)** — first SIS-targeting malware capable of catastrophic physical effects.
- **2018 Olympic Destroyer (Sandworm)** — false-flag tradecraft, Pyeongchang Games.
- **2020 SolarWinds / SUNBURST (APT29 / SVR)** — most strategically significant supply-chain compromise.
- **Feb 2022 Viasat KA-SAT / AcidRain (GRU)** — invasion-day satellite disruption with major European spillover.
- **2022 wiper waves (HermeticWiper, IsaacWiper, CaddyWiper, AcidRain, Industroyer2, WhisperGate)** — densest concentration of destructive nation-state operations on record.
- **2023 Snake takedown (Operation MEDUSA, Turla)** — first major US-led offensive disruption of an FSB tool.
- **2023–24 Midnight Blizzard at Microsoft (SVR)** — identity-provider strategic compromise.
- **2024 CARR water-utility HMI manipulation** — performative deterrence in US critical infrastructure; Treasury attribution to GRU.

## Sourcing Notes

Russia is the best-documented state cyber program in primary US/UK/EU government sources. DOJ has issued at least seven public indictments naming GRU, FSB, and TsNIIKhM officers (2017 Yahoo; Jul 2018 and Oct 2018 GRU; 2020 Sandworm; 2022 Berserk Bear + Triton; 2023 Star Blizzard; 2024–25 ongoing). Treasury OFAC has sanctioned the GRU's GTsST, FSB Center 16, TsNIIKhM, and named individuals across all three services and the proxy ecosystem. The Five Eyes attribution pattern (NotPetya Feb 2018, Olympic Destroyer Oct 2020, Viasat May 2022) is the canonical example of coordinated public attribution. ESET, Mandiant, Microsoft MSTIC, SentinelLabs, and Dragos have collectively published exhaustive technical reporting; ESET in particular has the deepest Sandworm-Ukraine technical lineage. NATO CCDCOE in Tallinn produces the most authoritative academic-policy work on international cyber law in the Russia context.

**Caveats and known gaps** worth flagging for the analyst layer:

1. The "Gerasimov doctrine" label has been retracted by the analyst who coined it (Mark Galeotti). AUSPEX should cite 2014 Military Doctrine and 2021 NSS for formal authority and use "Gerasimov framework" only as descriptive shorthand.
2. The 2007 Estonia and 2008 Georgia events have strong circumstantial but not legally-proven state attribution — flag confidence accordingly.
3. The Yahoo case (2014–16, indicted 2017) is sometimes underweighted in modern threat models but remains the only DOJ-indicted FSB strategic collection campaign with successful prosecution (Baratov).
4. Aliquippa, PA water hack (Nov 2023) was **CyberAv3ngers / IRGC (Iran)**, not Russia — frequently misattributed in popular reporting; the Russian (CARR) water-utility intrusions were Muleshoe, Abernathy, and Stanton, TX (Jan 2024) plus Indiana wastewater.
5. The Romanian election annulment (Dec 2024) attribution is strong inferred from declassified CSAT material but not legally adjudicated — flag as strongly inferred until further primary disclosure.
6. The post-2022 hacktivist front layer (XakNet, Anonymous Russia, Solntsepyok, RaHDIt) often produces unverifiable claims; only the Treasury/DOJ-attributed CARR and NoName057(16) operators are high-confidence linked to the state.
