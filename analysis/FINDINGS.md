# FINDINGS — numeric ledger for the who×why analyses

**Purpose.** A reproducible record of every number the `analysis/` scripts produce, each with
its exact reproduce command and the method caveats that bound it. This is a **verification aid
and planning scaffold, not dissertation prose.** Every `Interpretation (Kara):` slot is left
blank deliberately — the reading, the argument, and the claims are the candidate's to write and
to verify independently. The descriptive summaries in `analysis/README.md` are tool
documentation, not the dissertation's interpretation.

**Snapshot.** Corpus at commit `f57666d` · 2026-05-31 · 815 events · 204 actors · 86 doctrines
(15 states) · 178 policy-actions · 1,269 sources. Numbers below were captured from this snapshot;
re-running on a changed corpus will move them.

**Reproduce (all).** System `python3` + `pyyaml`, from repo root:
```sh
python3 analysis/doctrine_to_operations.py
python3 analysis/mo_narrowing.py
python3 analysis/doctrine_trends.py
python3 analysis/deterrence.py
python3 analysis/actor_deterrence.py
```
Every figure is regenerated by the one command named in its section. Independent of the TS engine.

## F1 · Do operations come out of strategic documents? (`doctrine_to_operations.py`)

and on **attested-only** links (a cited source — not the analyst — names the strategic goal).
Population: 728 operational events.

|---|---|---|---|
| 1 Legibility | ops carrying a doctrine link | 608/728 (**83%**) | 214/728 (**29%**) |
| 2 Precedence | op↔dated-doctrine pairs (n) | 617 | 219 |
| | median gap (op_year − doctrine_year) | **+3 yr** | **+3 yr** |
| | follow / same-year / precede | 73% / 8% / 18% | 75% / 10% / 13% |
| 3 Information | H(actor) → H(actor\|doctrine) | 6.50 → **2.25** bits | 5.40 → **1.61** bits |
| | mutual information | 4.25 bits | 3.79 bits |
| | actor-uncertainty resolved | **65%** | **70%** |
| | effective suspect pool | **91 → 5** actors | **42 → 3** actors |
| | over | 62 doctrines, 148 actors | 43 doctrines, 67 actors |

Most actor-determining doctrines (H(actor\|doc) ≈ 0): Hunt Forward Ops→Cyber National Mission
Force; LII→Storm-1516; 14th Five-Year Plan→China-aligned Taiwan semi cluster; Dual
Circulation & 15th Five-Year Plan→APT41; Russia NSS→Star Blizzard; Begin Doctrine &
Counter-Resistance-Axis→Olympic Games team. Most generic (high H, shared widely):
Sanctions-Response Posture (H 4.27, top REvil 10%), 2017 National Intelligence Law (3.56,
Mustang Panda 22%), Military-Civil Fusion (3.54, APT41 24%), Intelligentized Warfare (3.44).
Exemplar attested chains (doc→op, gap ≤10yr): NATO Ultimatum 2021→APT28 (2022, +1);
Defend Forward 2018→Iran-retaliation (2019, +1); 8th Congress 2021→Citrine Sleet/3CX (2022, +1);
Info-Security Doctrine 2016→Berserk Bear (2018, +2); Hunt Forward 2018→Estonia (2020, +2);
Nat Cyber Security Strategy 2016→Sandworm (2019, +3). Stuxnet: "Operation Olympic Games /
Stuxnet" (2007, `us/olympic-games-team`) ← Begin Doctrine (IL, 1981, strongly_inferred) + Title
10/50 (US, 2018, strongly_inferred); a second "Stuxnet" (2009, **actor null**) ← Asymmetric
Warfare Doctrine (IR, attested).

**Method caveats (factual):** observational; cannot prove generation. Confounds: reverse
codification (doctrine written to describe existing behaviour), analyst-mediated tagging
(addressed by the attested-only cut), common-cause geopolitics. Year-granular; legibility partly
reflects corpus coverage.

**Open questions to address:** Is +3yr precedence evidence of generation, or consistent with
codification-after-the-fact? Why does the attested-only cut narrow *more*, not less? Does the
two-record Stuxnet modelling (esp. the null-actor 2009 record tagged with Iran's doctrine) need
correcting? — see also `verify_atlas.py doctrine-state-mismatch`.

**C. Incremental chain** (nested, your causal order; permutation null, K=40):

| conditioning on | H(act\|·) | null | naive pool | REAL pool | REAL bits | Δreal |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| (nothing) | 6.73 | 6.73 | 106 | 106 | 0.00 | — |
| + doctrine | 2.32 | 3.73 | 5.0 | **40.1** | 1.40 | +1.40 |

Sparsity at the deepest level: 236 distinct (doc,sec,MO) cells, **143 singletons (60%)**, mean
2.3 events/cell.

**Method caveats (factual):** **circularity** — analysts use MO/target/doctrine *to* attribute,
so feature→actor is partly definitional (measures stereotypy, not independent evidence).
Sparsity at deep conditioning handled by the permutation null — trust REAL, not naive, drops; the
null-corrected estimate itself is unstable once cells are mostly singletons (the negative Δreal
is the symptom). `false_flag_risk≠none` excluded. Primary-feature simplification (first
doctrine / primary-target sector / terminal-outcome bucket). Year-granular; collection-biased.

**Open questions to address:** Does the naive 106→2 vs REAL 106→40 gap change how attribution
confidence should be reported? Is MO's value genuinely tail-concentrated (KP-money) rather than
general? How much of the doctrine→actor link is circular given analyst tagging?

## F4 · State-level deterrence / naming-and-shaming (`deterrence.py`)

Event-study + difference-in-differences. 73 of 82 punitive policy-actions (sanction / indictment
/ export-control / asset-seizure) had usable ±2yr windows. DiD<0 = target decelerated vs the
world after the action (deterrence-consistent); >0 = accelerated (defiance).

| target state | #punitive | mean post/pre (target) | mean DiD |
|---|---|---|---|
| ru | 26 | 1.48× | −0.04 |
| cn | 24 | 1.11× | +0.39 |
| kp | 14 | 1.16× | +0.23 |
| ir | 9 | 1.07× | +0.09 |

**Aggregate:** mean DiD **+0.17** · median +0.05 · 46% deceleration-vs-world, 53% acceleration.

**Method caveats (factual):** observational, not causal. **Endogeneity** — punitive actions
respond to op surges, so pre-windows are elevated by construction, biasing *toward* apparent
post-action mean-reversion (false "deterrence"); the observed acceleration runs *against* that
bias. State-level (policy-actions target states, not actors); year-granular; overlapping windows;
collection coverage.

**Open questions to address:** Given endogeneity biases toward false deterrence, what does
observed *acceleration* imply? Is the per-state spread (cn +0.39 vs ru −0.04) signal or noise?

## F5 · Actor-level deterrence (`actor_deterrence.py`)

The sharper cut — does naming a *specific* actor change *its* tempo? Indictments/call-outs bridged
to actors via meta events carrying `actor_id`/`operators_named`; same event-study + DiD on the
actor's own ops (rest-of-other-actors control; right-censored namings dropped).

- **Usable before/after windows: N=9 actors** (2 first-named by indictment, 7 by disclosure;
  2 more dropped as right-censored).
- **Aggregate:** all mean DiD **−0.71**, median −0.88, 77% decelerated. Indictment-first n=2:
  −0.87. Disclosure-first n=7: −0.66.
- **Endogeneity diagnostic:** 4/9 actors were named in/just-after their single busiest op-year;
  mean DiD named-at-peak **−1.31** vs off-peak **−0.23** (n=5).
- **Cross-check:** the actor-level −0.71 contradicts the state-level +0.17 (F4).

**Method caveats (factual):** **underpowered + confounded** — N=9, per-actor op counts 1–4,
DiD parallel-trends violated by construction (naming triggered by the actor's own surge). The
peak-vs-off-peak split (−1.31 vs −0.23) localises the apparent "deterrence" to regression to the
mean. The state level (F4) has more events per unit and a cleaner control. Retained as a worked
example of recognising and rejecting a confounded positive result.

**Open questions to address:** Is the actor-level cut reportable at all, or only as a
methodological cautionary case? Does the sign-flip vs F4 settle which granularity to trust?
